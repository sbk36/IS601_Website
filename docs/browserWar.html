Browser Wars

A group of college students was working on making the internet more user-friendly so that anyone may use it without needing to be a computer genius, by adding visuals and photos, as well as audio and video capabilities. Online mosaic - Jim Clark, co-founder of Netscape, approached Marc Andreessen who is a co-creator of mosaic, a new graduate student, about creating a new software firm. They spent a lot of time attempting to transform mosaic into a profitable business.

 

Bill Gates monopolized the entire computer world, and he believed that this would be one of those tech companies that would have to pay Microsoft just to get started. In 1975, Bill Gates embarked on his road to becoming a technological icon. He dropped out of Harvard at the age of 19 and formed a partnership with Paul Allan. Bill Gates' ambition was simple but ambitious: every office and home would have a personal computer. He was on the verge of monopolizing the computing world in 1993. Microsoft was installed on approximately 90% of PCs, netting him billions of dollars.

 

In the summer of 1994, Jim Clark founded Netscape Communications, the world's fastest software firm. Netscape Navigator was the first browser to be released, and it competed with Microsoft Windows. This film depicted a battle between Microsoft, the world's greatest computer firm, and a tiny group of computer students who developed a new technology.
