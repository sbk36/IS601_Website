People

Some analysts feel that the internet's purpose is to bring people together. Is the internet formed by what people desire, or is it using money to guide our population in specific directions? Music sharing became one of the most popular online venues in the 1990s. The invention of the mp3 format made it easy to exchange music over the internet. Napster was the largest of these platforms. Within a year, millions of people were sharing music on the internet, posing legal issues for the music industry. This resulted in one of the most significant legal battles ever fought over the internet.

 

Napster was accused of stealing music by allowing users to share it, which violated copyright rules, according to artists and music publishers.Finally, the Supreme Court determined that Napster had broken copyright rules and ordered that it be stopped down immediately. This simply resulted in the birth of new businesses such as Apple Music and YouTube. Platforms like YouTube have given users a whole new way to see their own work. This gave birth to a new web 2.0 generation.

 

People all around the world could communicate and, more importantly, be seen due to platforms like Myspace. Soon after, Facebook was launched, and its user base developed at such a rapid rate that it spawned a completely new way of using the internet. Humans' need for social growth is the cause of this expansion. With these needs for social acceptance and fame, social media platforms have grown to be larger than firms like IBM, which have been operating for decades. Companies like Myspace and Facebook make billions of dollars merely from advertising on their platforms. With more users and more time spent online, the web continues to grow. What the web will bring next is still an open subject.
