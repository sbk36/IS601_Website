Internet Search

Part two of "The True Story of the Internet" gives us a historical perspective on life before Google. How difficult it was to just discover things on the internet, and how people had to click on links after links in the hopes of finding a simple solution. Almost all-important technology, software, and internet companies, as well as clever computer experts, are based in Silicon Valley. It establishes a hub for innovative businesses to grow and succeed. Silicon Valley is home to major corporations such as Intel, Apple, and Oracle.

 

Yahoo originated when two students were looking for sports information from Stanford University in order to win a basketball game. Jerry Yang and David Filo were the two students. Yahoo later found that the internet may be used to produce money. However, just like any other business, a competitor named "Excite" emerged. Excite was a search engine start-up that was quite similar to Yahoo and that it was also formed by college students. The only difference was that the technology that Excite built was deemed sophisticated.

 

Google, like practically every other tech business, was founded by two Stanford University undergraduates in California. The search engine was not performing well at the moment. As previously stated, the search engine proved ineffective. Users would type a word, and a list of links would appear, either leading to further links or to a link to purchase items they didn't want. Google eventually perfected the search engine, gathering information from the internet based on a single word typed into the search box. Google's search engine speeds up the updating of results, making the indexing method quick and adaptable.
